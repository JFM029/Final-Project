How the Solar System was Created

The formation of the solar story that spans billions of years. Scientitst believe it began around 4.6 billion years ago with the collapse of a giatn molecular cloud in a region of space Known as the solar nebula. .

Solar Nebula

The solar system started out as a massive cloud of gas and dust called the solar nebula. This nebula was made up of hydrogen, helium, and heavire elements produced by earlier generation of stars. The collapse of the nebula might have been triggerd by the shockwave from a nearby supernova explosion, compressing the gas and dust into a denser region.

Formation of the Sun

As the Nebula collasped under its gravity, most of the material was drawn toward the center, forming a protosun. The protsun grew hotter and denser until nuclear fusion began, igniting the Sun and making it the star we know today. The Sun's strong gravitational pull kept the rest of the material in orbit around it.

Creation of the Protoplanetary Disk

The remaining gas and dust flattened into a spinning disk around thte sun, called the protoplantary disk. Withink this disk, particles of dust and ice began to stick together, forming small clumps known as planetesimals.

Formation of Planets

Over time, these planetsimals collided and combined, growing into larger vodies called protplanes. The Sun's intense heat caused lighter elements like hydrgoen and helium to be pushed outward, forming the gas giants Juipter, Urnaus, and Neptune in the outer solar system. Closer to the Sun, heavier, rcoky materials formed the terrestrial planets Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars.

Leftover Materials

Not all the material in the solar nebula formed planets. Some became:

  • Asteroids in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter
  • Comets in regions like the Kuiper Belt and the Oort Cloud
  • Moons orbiting planets, formed from debris or captured planestesimals

The Role of Gravity

Gravity played a crucial role in shaping the solar system. It organized planets into stable orbits and pulled smaller objects into planets, moons, or the Sun.
